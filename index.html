ABOUT MADURAI

The medieval city of Madurai was the capital city in the Pandiyan kingdom and is now known for its historical monuments like Meenakshi Amman temple and Thirumalai Nayakar mahal. The Meenakshi Amman temple complex acted as the town centre during the Pandiyan reign and is now geographically and culturally situated at the heart of the city. Being a city with a healthy mix of ancient and the new, Madurai gives an unique experience to its visitors.

This blog aims to give you suggestions on how you can make your stay a memorable one. As you are visiting Madurai in the mid of May, you can explore things from Mango to Jackfruit, from the scorching heat to a cold glass of Jigarthanda. Here is the list of things that we recommend you to do(places to eat and wander) in and around Madurai.

Where to Wander

Meenakshi Amman Temple area

schedule Recommended time : after 5pm. would take around 2-3 hours

Though it's a bit crowded, we recommend you to visit in the evening to experience the bustling life of the city. The streets around the temple are named after the month names of the Tamil calendar, starting with chithirai.

Don‚Äôt forget to visit these following places when you visit the temple:

  • Aayiram kaal mandapam - Located after entering through the west gate
  • Pond - Located in the westside inside the temple
  • Snack stall - Ask temple employee for directions
  • Pudhu mandapam - Outside the complex - sells things like craft goods, tunics, cheap ornaments etc.,
  • Walk along the chithirai streets(vehicle free area)
  • Refer to street food section for food suggestions and consider a walk to Thirumalai Nayakar mahal


Azhagar kovil area

schedule 4-5 hours including the journey from Madurai(Early mornings recommended)

you could start as early as 5 in the morning to enjoy a trek in the hills.

Things to see:

  • Kall azhagar temple located at the base of the hill
  • Pazhamudhir cholai(Murugan temple) on top of the hill
  • a few monkeys
  • Stop for a snack or lunch at Hawa valley(20 min detour)

If you have more time to spend, visit hotel kadambavanam (It has a good view) or drive to Sirumalai


Thirumalai Nayakkar mahal

schedule 9 am - 8:30 pm (breaks 1-1:30 pm, 5-6 pm)

This architectural wonder is another important historic site that was constructed in the early 17th century. The huge darbar halls with humongous pillars is a pleasure to watch. Director Maniratnam has patronised this place and has featured it in such movies as Guru, Bombay and Iruvar. The sound and light show is recommended. We would suggest you to wear mosquito repellents.



Thiruparankundram

schedule 8 am - 12:30 pm and 4:00 - 7:30 pm

The temple is built in rock-cut architecture and believed to have been built by the Pandyas during the 6th century.


Mariamman Teppakulam

schedule 9:00 am - 6:00 pm

The largest Tank in Tamil Nadu. Take a boat ride to the temple surrounded by water and savour the street food alongside.
