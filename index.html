ABOUT MADURAI

The medieval city of Madurai was the capital city in the Pandiyan kingdom and is now known for its historical monuments like Meenakshi Amman temple and Thirumalai nayakar mahal. The Meenakshi amman temple complex acted as the town centre during the Pandiyan reign and is now geographically and culturally situated at the heart of the city. Being a city with a healthy mix of ancient and the new, Madurai gives an unique experience to its visitors.

This blog aims to give you suggestions on how you can make your stay a memorable one. As you are visiting Madurai in the mid of May, you can explore things from Mango to Jackfruit, from the scorching heat to a cold glass of Jigarthanda. Here is the list of things that we recommend you to do(places to eat and wander) in and around Madurai.

Roadside vendors around the temple

Must try!

You can spot a lot of roadside vendors around the temple selling various stuff such as fresh fruits, kadala and other snacks.

When to visit?

from 6:00 pm to 8:30 pm

what to buy?

Jackfruit, boiled peanuts, thennanguruthu(coconut stem), green mangoes, Saurashtra vada vendors (try mullumurugai vadai or Poli)etc.,

Location

You can find a number of such vendors on a walk(15 - 20 mins) from Meenakshi Amman temple (from pudhu mandapam) to Thirumalai nayakar mahal.


Famous Jigarthanda

Must try!

Jigarthanda is a famous drink unique to Madurai. It is prepared using Milk, China grass, Sarsaparilla syrup and Ice-cream.

When to visit?

Whenever you feel like indulging in a creamy cold dessert!

Remarks

We would advise you to get it in a disposable cup to avoid the crowd and for better hygiene.

Konar kadai

Non.Veg

Must try!

There are two konar kadais: one in Simmakkal and the other in Anna nagar. If you are closer to the wedding venue, you can visit the Anna nagar shop and if you are closer to Meenakshi Amman temple, you can visit the Simmakkal shop.

What to buy?

Kari dosai(signature meat dosai) which we recommend you share between 2-3 people, yennai dosai + egg poriyal.

Remarks

Please note that the Simmakkal branch may be a bit noisy and crowded.

Vista Restaurant - Gateway Taj Hotel

Highly Recommended!

Sitting on the top of Pasumalai Hill, The Gateway Hotel stretches over 62 acres of peaceful landscaped gardens. If you are in a mood to loosen up your purse strings and have a good time, this is the place to go. Food and drinks are much recommended and the view is spectacular. If you don’t have the time for dinner, you can at least pay a visit for a cup of tea or an early breakfast. You can clearly see how much we love the view. It would cost you around 1.5k per person for food and further 1k for drinks. Prior table reservation may be required for lunch or dinner.

Remarks

Got a lot of peacocks! you may be lucky to spot a few!!
